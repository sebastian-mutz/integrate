Atmospheric Pressure
--------------------

Before we explore any specifics, we familiarise ourselves with a few important concepts related to pressure.

Partial Pressures
.................

.. math:: T_{red} = T_{blue}

.. math:: P_t = T_{red} + T_{blue}


Ideal Gas Law
.............

.. math:: 

    \begin{equation}
      P · V = n · \bar{R} · T
    \end{equation}         
    

Mass Density
............

.. math:: 

    \begin{equation}
      \rho = \frac {n · M} {V}
    \end{equation}         
    


Specific Gas Constant
.....................

.. math:: 

    \begin{equation}
      R = \frac {\bar{R}} {M}
    \end{equation}     


"Air"
.....

.. math:: M_{air} \approx 29

.. math:: R_{air} \approx 287 Jkg^{-1}K^{-1}


Ideal Gas Law (modified)
........................
   
.. math:: 

    \begin{equation}
      P = \rho · R · T
    \end{equation}        
   
   
.. math:: R \approx 287 Jkg^{-1}K^{-1}   
.. math:: T \approx 19°C = 292.15 K      
.. math:: \rho \approx 1.2985 kgm^{-3}        
   
.. math:: 

    \begin{equation}
      P_{troposphere} = 1.2985 kgm^{-3} · 287 Jkg^{-1}K^{-1} · 292.15 K 
      \approx 108900 Pa
    \end{equation}           
   
Buoyancy
--------

To understand vertical transport, mixing and precipitation formation in the atmosphere, we need to first familiarise ourselves with the buoyancy force.
   
   
Buoyancy Force
..............

.. math:: 

    \begin{equation}
      B = V · \rho · g
    \end{equation}           



Buoyancy Force: Example I
.........................

.. math:: P_p = P_e


.. math:: 

    \begin{equation}
      P = \rho · R · T
    \end{equation}     
    
.. math:: 

    \begin{equation}
      P_e = \frac {P_e} {R · T_e} 
      = \frac {101000 Pa} {287 Jkg^{-1}K^{-1} · 278 K} 
      = 1.266 kgm^{-3}
    \end{equation}         

    
.. math:: 

    \begin{equation}
      P_p = \frac {P_p} {R · T_p} 
      = \frac {101000 Pa} {287 Jkg^{-1}K^{-1} · 288 K} 
      = 1.222 kgm^{-3}
    \end{equation}             

    
Buoyancy Force: Example II (changing chemistry)
...............................................

.. math:: P_p = P_e
.. math:: T_p = T_e
    
.. math:: 

    \begin{equation}
      P = \rho · R · T
    \end{equation}       
    
.. math:: 

    \begin{equation}
      B = V · \rho · g
    \end{equation}           
    

Pressure and Altitude
---------------------


Barometric Formula
..................

.. math:: 

    \begin{equation}
      P_h = P_0 · e ^{- \frac {h · m · g} {R · T} } 
    \end{equation}           

    
Hydrostatic Balance
...................

.. math:: 

    \begin{equation}
      P · A = m · g
    \end{equation}           

.. math::     
    \begin{equation}
      \frac {P} {g} = \frac {m} {A}
    \end{equation}           
    
.. math::     
    \begin{equation}
      P_G - P_T = \rho · \Delta z · g
    \end{equation}
