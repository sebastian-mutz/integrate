Building a Climate VI¶

Coriolis Force

This lecture introduces the Coriolis force, the geostrophic balance and their implications for climate.

Information¶

Learning goals

  • Understand the Coriolis force and how to calculate it

  • Understand the geostrophic balance and how it relates to wind speeds

Coriolis Force¶

\[\begin{equation} F_c = 2 · m · u · \Omega · sin(\phi) \end{equation}\]
\[\begin{equation} \Omega = \frac {2 · \pi (rad)} {60 · 60 · 24 (s)} = 2.27 · 10^{-5}s^{-1} \end{equation}\]

Geostrophic Balance and Wind Speeds¶

\[\begin{equation} F_p = \frac {\Delta P}{\Delta} · V = \nabla P · V \end{equation}\]
\[\begin{equation} F_c = 2 · m · u · \Omega · sin(\phi) \end{equation}\]
\[m = \rho · V\]
\[F_p = F_c\]
\[\begin{equation} \nabla P · V = 2 · \rho · V · u · \Omega · sin(\phi) \end{equation}\]
\[\begin{equation} u_g = \frac {\nabla P} {2 · \rho · \Omega · sin(\phi) } \end{equation}\]

Geostrophic Balance: Example Problem¶

\[\begin{equation} \nabla P = \frac {1 · 10^2 Pa}{1 · 10^5m} = 1 · 10^{-3} Pa m^{-1} \end{equation}\]
\[\begin{equation} u_g = \frac {1 · 10^{-3} Pa m^{-1}} {2 · 1.2 kgm^{-3} · 2.27 · 10^{-5}s{-1} · sin(28.5) } \approx 18.7 ms^{-1} \end{equation}\]
